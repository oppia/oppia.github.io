The Exploration Editor

After you've clicked the "Create Exploration" button, you'll be taken to the exploration editor page.

At first, your exploration will contain a single card, called "First Card". Cards are the building blocks for your explorations. You can think of cards as the places a learner (anybody who views your exploration) can potentially reach.

As you continue creating and expanding your exploration, you'll be adding to the number of cards, all of which will be visible in the Exploration Overview (also in the picture above). The editing interface only shows one card at a time, and you can navigate to other cards by clicking on them in the Exploration Overview.

Parts of a Card

A card consists of three parts: some content, an interaction (or question), and responses that specify what Oppia should do next, based on the learner's answer.

Content

The card's content is an editable block of text.

Here, you can write whatever you want the learner to read. The content can be about any topic/subject, and would end with a question or statement that invites the learner to interact with Oppia. You can also include rich-text elements like images, videos and links.

Interaction

This is the type of activity that the learner performs while learning. It could be typing in a text field, placing a marker on a map, or various other things.

Note: Oppia provides a number of different interactions out of the box, but if you have an idea for an interaction that doesn't already exist, please file an issue on GitHub describing it and what you'd like it to do. This allows us to keep track of the request, and someone might be able to help you build it in the future. You'd also be welcome to try building it yourself, and contributing it to the open source codebase.

Responses

Once you've chosen an interaction, you can decide what Oppia should do, based on the learner's answer. Different interaction types will have different sets of response options to choose from. You can provide feedback and then have the learner redo the interaction, or move them to a different card altogether.

Hints

Once you've specified what Oppia should do for answers, you can specify hints for the current card. You can add a list of hints that the learner can use when stuck. To prevent learners from abusing this feature, a hint can be requested only if one of the following conditions are satisfied:

  1. The learner submitted a wrong answer at least once.
  2. It has been more than 30 seconds since the learner last submitted an answer.
